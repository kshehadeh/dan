My career started with an internship in 1995 making some pretty awful websites for the American History museum. It was an amazing, career-orienting experience and dantrachtman.com launched just a few months later.

I headed to San Francisco in 1998, during the height of the boom, joining the flood of dotcomers overpaid and driving up rent prices. I did a lot of freelance work and traveled in between gigs. In 2000, I was fortunate to find myself reading French theory in an MFA in Design program, while the industry seemed to implode and many friends with formerly impressive job titles found themselves waiting tables.

After graduating in 2002, I headed back to the DC and took a job as an art director at AOL, only to find it couldn't be taken down from the inside. I spent a couple years working with some really smart people at the National Gallery of Art and then 2 more at the Library of Congress. At some point in between, I was talked into teaching graphic design and Photoshop and found I was even half decent at it.

I am now leaving my post at NASDAQ to do more traveling, this time with a wife to keep me out of trouble. We plan to spend 6 months meandering through South America, working on our Spanish and exploring ruins with 20 character names. Thanks for visiting, and feel free to shoot me an email.
